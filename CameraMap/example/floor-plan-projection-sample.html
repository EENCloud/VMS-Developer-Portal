Eagle Eye Networks — projecting devicePosition onto a floor plan

This single file demonstrates why a naïve interpretation of a floor's two corner coordinates does not match the VMS, and the similarity transform that does. All data below is from GET /…/floors and GET /cameras?include=devicePosition for "Capital Factory Floor 5". ❌ Naïve bilinear interpolation (longitude → x, latitude → y) — does NOT match the VMS

Independent per-axis stretch. Because the geo box is portrait (~21.5 m × 108 m) but the image is landscape (1588×460), cameras smear across the wrong axis and several land outside the plan (shown in red).

✅ Similarity transform (uniform scale + rotation + reflection) — matches the VMS

The two corners are a diagonal; combined with the image pixel dimensions they define a similarity transform. A reflection is required because image pixels are y-down while geographic north is up. Field-of-view wedges use the same transform for direction.
